Well, nothing much to say about Jupiter itself as the gas giant isn’t inhabitable at all.

Known as the largest planet, but iIts actual size is about 10 times smaller than Earth's. 90% of its massive look is liquid hydrogen that circulates around small rock core, so taking a smoke isn’t a good idea on Jupiter. And hydrogens mass would squish you as soon as you leave your spaceship.

Practically habitable zone on Jupiter isn’t actually on planet itself, it’s on one of 80 moons. Scientists think that best would be Europa. Ice covered moon with oceans beneath it.

It is believed that its oceans might have some kind of a life form hidden in it.

As for living there, it’s an ice giant, its freezing! Averaging -200 degrees Celsius. All around thick layer of ice and only thing that’s visible is a sort of ripples about 2 meters tall.

You might have trouble with deadly radiation and icequakes, that shoot masses of freezing cold water out of ice cracks, but some of it could be avoided if you set up a base camp on part of Europa, that is facing movement of the planet.

Gravity would be approximate the same as moons, so lots of fun but the view from Europa would be outstanding, huge Jupiter and other 80 moons orbiting it, clearly visible as there is practically no atmosphere to block the view. Perfect spot for proposal!
